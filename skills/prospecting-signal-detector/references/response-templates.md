# Response Templates

Use these product-agnostic engagement templates after loading the user's product context, sector
context, approved positioning, and signal criteria from text or files.

## Pain Signal Response

When someone describes a problem the user's context says they can credibly address:

Approach: Acknowledge -> Insight -> Open question. Do not pitch in Touch 1.

Example framework:

> That problem shows up a lot when [specific condition from their post] meets [context from the
> user's brief]. One practical way teams handle it is [genuine useful insight]. How are you
> approaching [specific aspect] today?

## Question Signal Response

When someone asks for tool, vendor, partner, or approach recommendations:

Approach: Honest overview -> Natural positioning -> Offer to continue.

Example framework:

> Depends on your setup. [Option A] tends to fit [use case]. [Option B] fits [different use case].
> If [condition from user-provided context] is central, there may be another path worth considering.
> Happy to share the tradeoffs if helpful.

## Trigger Event Response

When you detect a role change, launch, funding, expansion, deadline, regulation, migration, or other
event from the user's signal criteria:

Approach: Congratulate or acknowledge -> Relevant resource or lesson -> Soft seed.

Example framework:

> Congrats on [event]. The first stretch after that usually exposes [relevant challenge from the
> brief]. One thing I have seen help is [useful insight]. Curious whether [specific issue] is already
> on your radar.

## Competitor or Alternative Mention

- If praising a competitor, do not argue. Add useful expertise or track as intelligence.
- If complaining, empathize with the specific pain and share a neutral way to think about solving it.
- If asking about alternatives, use the Question Signal template.

## The 3-Touch Sequence

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Day 0 | Public comment | Thoughtful value-add comment, no product mention |
| 2 | Day 2-3 | Their next post or DM | Engage again or send a brief message referencing the exchange |
| 3 | Day 5-7 | Direct message | Personalized message with a specific resource or offer approved by the user |

## Hashtag Strategy for Signal-Derived Posts

Use hashtags only for original LinkedIn posts, never comments.

Choose 3-5 hashtags from the user's context:

- 1-2 broad category hashtags.
- 1-2 audience, problem, or job-to-be-done hashtags.
- 1 niche community, event, geography, or campaign hashtag.

Place hashtags at the end of the post, separated by a blank line.

## Rules

- Never pitch in Touch 1.
- Lead with genuine value.
- Record every engagement action in the signal database.
- Never repeat an action already in the database.
- Always show original post URLs for verification before engaging.
- Always include relevant hashtags on original LinkedIn posts, not comments.
- Offer scheduling only when the user asks to publish or schedule a post.
