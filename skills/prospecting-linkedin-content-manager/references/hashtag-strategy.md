# Hashtag Research & Strategy Guide

## Hashtag Selection Methodology

### Step 1: Build Your Hashtag Library

For each content pillar, research and maintain a library of hashtags across three tiers:

**Tier 1 — Broad Reach (>500K followers):**
Purpose: Maximum exposure, highly competitive
Examples: broad category, role, or movement hashtags from the user's sector context.
Use: 1 per post maximum

**Tier 2 — Targeted (10K-500K followers):**
Purpose: Reach relevant professionals, moderate competition
Examples: audience, job-to-be-done, product category, or problem-space hashtags from the user's
provided context.
Use: 1-2 per post

**Tier 3 — Niche (<10K followers):**
Purpose: Community building, low competition, high relevance
Examples: niche community, use-case, location, event, or buyer-problem hashtags from the user's
provided context.
Use: 1-2 per post

### Step 2: Research Hashtag Performance

For each hashtag candidate:
1. Search the hashtag on LinkedIn
2. Note the follower count
3. Review the quality of recent posts (are they relevant? spammy?)
4. Check if your target audience follows this hashtag
5. Test if your posts appear in the hashtag feed

### Step 3: Create Hashtag Groups

Build pre-defined hashtag sets for each content pillar:

**Example for one pillar:**
- Set A: [broad category] [audience role] [problem space] [topic] [community]
- Set B: [category] [use case] [market shift] [method] [community]
- Set C: [adjacent category] [tooling or method] [outcome] [buyer role] [niche topic]

Rotate sets to avoid repetition and test which combinations perform best.

### Step 4: Track and Optimize

Maintain a hashtag performance log:

| Hashtag | Tier | Posts Used | Avg Reach | Avg Engagement | Keep/Drop |
|---------|------|-----------|-----------|----------------|-----------|

Review monthly. Drop underperformers, test new hashtags regularly.

## Hashtag Placement Rules

- Always place hashtags at the END of the post
- Separate from the body with a line break
- Never embed hashtags in body text (looks spammy)
- 3-5 hashtags is optimal. More than 5 is counterproductive.
- Don't use trending but irrelevant hashtags (algorithm penalizes this)

## Branded Hashtags

Create one branded hashtag for recurring content:
- Include the user's name, company, project, or campaign name.
- Use consistently to build a searchable content archive
- Encourage followers to use it
- Feature it in your profile/featured section

## Seasonal & Trending Hashtags

Monitor and leverage:
- Industry event hashtags (conferences, product launches)
- Annual observances (tech weeks, innovation days)
- Breaking news related hashtags (use only if genuinely relevant)
- LinkedIn's own trending topics
