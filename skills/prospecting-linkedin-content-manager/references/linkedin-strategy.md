# LinkedIn Strategy Reference

Use this reference when defining LinkedIn goals, content pillars, cadence, audience targets, and
growth strategy.

## Content Flywheel

1. Attract: Hook-driven content that earns attention.
2. Engage: Value-rich content that earns comments, saves, and shares.
3. Convert: Strategic CTAs that turn readers into followers, leads, or connections.
4. Learn: Analytics-driven iteration that compounds results over time.

## Content Pillar Architecture

Define 3-5 pillars at the intersection of:

- User expertise: what the user knows deeply.
- Audience needs: problems the audience actively faces.
- Market relevance: current trends, industry pressure, or timely debates.

Each pillar needs a clear theme, 10+ subtopics, target output percentage, and strategic goal.

## Content Mix

- 50% value posts: insights, frameworks, how-tos.
- 20% story posts: experience, failures, lessons.
- 15% engagement posts: polls, questions, debates, hot takes.
- 10% authority posts: achievements, press, speaking, partnerships.
- 5% promotional posts: direct product or service mentions.

## Growth Levers

- Post consistently 3-5 times per week.
- Comment strategically on larger relevant accounts for 10-15 minutes daily.
- Engage with commenters within 1 hour of posting.
- Cross-pollinate with newsletters, podcasts, communities, or other channels.
- Use collaborative posts and mentions when they are genuinely relevant.

## Profile Optimization

- Headline states a value proposition, not only a job title.
- About section is story-driven and clear about who the user helps.
- Featured section includes the best posts or resources.
- Banner is on brand.
- Creator topics match the content pillars.

## Metrics Hierarchy

1. Awareness: impressions, followers.
2. Resonance: engagement rate, comments, saves.
3. Conversion: profile views, connection requests, DMs, leads.
4. Business impact: deals, partnerships, attributed opportunities.

Benchmark engagement rates:

- Below 1%: needs improvement.
- 1-3%: average.
- 3-5%: good.
- 5-10%: excellent.
- 10%+: viral territory.
