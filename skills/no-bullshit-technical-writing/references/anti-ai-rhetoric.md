# Anti-AI Rhetoric Checklist

Core principle: form follows function. Every stylistic choice must serve the content.

## Accuracy

- Never invent personal anecdotes, fake relationships, customers, studies, numbers, or production
  results.
- Mark hypothetical examples as hypothetical.
- Use real, verifiable names, dates, companies, code paths, metrics, or studies when available.
- Take clear positions when evidence is solid. Use qualifiers only for genuine uncertainty.

## Avoid

- Hyperbole: groundbreaking, revolutionary, transformative, unprecedented, incredible, amazing,
  profound, remarkable, epic.
- Dramatic abstractions: paradox, trap, illusion, magic, destiny, chaos, apocalypse, miracle.
- Weak intensifiers: clearly, obviously, really, actually, literally, particularly, essentially,
  fundamentally, inherently.
- Business filler: synergy, leverage, paradigm shift, game-changer, disruption, thought leadership,
  move the needle, low-hanging fruit, bandwidth, scalable mindset, best practices, solutions.
- Empty phrases: it is worth noting, at the end of the day, the fact of the matter is, needless to
  say, what is interesting is that.
- Pseudo-punchlines: The result:, The point is:, The real question is:, The bottom line:, Here's the
  thing:.
- Rhetorical formulas: "Not X, but Y", "It's not... but...", "X isn't... it's...".
- No em dashes, multiple punctuation, emojis, hashtags, or decorative formatting.

## Openings

Avoid broad claims about "today's world", obvious definitions, historical preambles, and empty
questions. Start with a specific observation, a concrete technical problem, a named example, a data
point, or the thesis.

Test: if the first paragraph can be removed without losing meaning, remove it.

## Rhythm

- In every 3 to 4 paragraphs, include at least one 30+ word sentence and one 5 to 8 word sentence.
- Avoid three or more consecutive sentences with nearly identical length or structure.
- Target a natural mix: about 20% short sentences, 50% medium sentences, and 30% long sentences.
- Vary sentence openings. Avoid several subject-verb openings in a row.

## Examples And Data

- Weave data into sentences: "Latency dropped from 800 ms to 240 ms" beats two separate fragments.
- Use examples to add information, not to repeat the same point in softer language.
- Mix scales when useful: one implementation detail, one team or company case, one market or
  architectural implication.
- Avoid generic placeholders such as "a company" or "research shows" when specifics are available.

## Closings

Close with a concrete implication, decision, risk, or next step. Do not restate the introduction or
drift into vague philosophy.
