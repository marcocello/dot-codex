# Topic Research Reference

Use this reference when researching selected LinkedIn content topics.

## Research Process

1. Run broad searches from 3-5 angles.
2. Search specifically for statistics, research papers, surveys, and benchmarks.
3. Find expert perspectives from credible operators, analysts, researchers, practitioners, or
   leaders in the user's provided sector context.
4. Look for counter-arguments and active debates.
5. Find real case studies from companies or people.
6. Search LinkedIn for similar topics to inspect angles, formats, and hashtags.
7. Check whether the topic is currently trending or tied to recent news.

## Research Brief Format

```markdown
## Research Brief: [Topic]

### Key Statistics & Data
- [Data point] - Source: [source]

### Expert Perspectives
- [Expert]: [quote or paraphrase] - Source: [source]

### Case Studies
- [Company/person]: [what happened and why it matters]

### Counter-Arguments & Debates
- [Opposing view]: [summary]

### Unique Angles for LinkedIn Posts
1. [Angle]: [why it would work]

### Suggested Hooks
1. [Hook]

### Relevant Hashtags
- [Hashtag] - [why it fits]

### People or Companies to Mention
- [Name]: [reason]

### Visual Ideas
- [Image, carousel, chart, or document idea]
```

## Quality Bar

- Source every statistic.
- Prefer recent data from the last 12-18 months.
- Include at least one contrarian perspective.
- Prioritize surprising or non-obvious evidence.
- Tie findings back to the user's content pillars and audience.
