# LinkedIn Algorithm & Optimization Guide

## How the LinkedIn Algorithm Works (2025-2026)

### Content Distribution Phases

**Phase 1: Quality Scoring (0-60 minutes)**
The algorithm evaluates content quality before broad distribution:
- Spam detection and classification
- Content categorization (topic, format)
- Initial quality score based on text analysis
- Creator credibility score (SSI, history)

**Phase 2: Test Audience (1-4 hours)**
Content is shown to a small test group:
- First-degree connections (~8-10% of network)
- Engagement rate in this window is critical
- Comments weighted 10-15x more than likes
- Saves and reposts are strong positive signals
- Dwell time (time spent reading) matters

**Phase 3: Extended Distribution (4-24 hours)**
If Phase 2 performs well:
- Second-degree connections (friends of friends)
- Topic followers (hashtag and topic feeds)
- Geographic and industry expansion
- Engagement velocity determines speed of expansion

**Phase 4: Long Tail (1-14 days)**
High-performing content continues distributing:
- Resurfaced in feeds intermittently
- "Trending" placement possible
- Newsletter and digest inclusion
- Search discoverability

### Algorithm Ranking Signals

**Positive signals (high weight):**
- Comments (especially multi-sentence, conversations)
- Shares with commentary
- Saves/bookmarks
- DMs sharing the post
- Engagement from outside your network
- Fast engagement in first 60 minutes

**Positive signals (medium weight):**
- Reactions (especially non-like: celebrate, insightful, love)
- Click-throughs on links (but links reduce reach — see below)
- Profile visits after viewing post
- Follow from post
- Dwell time (long reads)

**Negative signals:**
- "I don't want to see this" clicks
- Unfollows after post
- Spam reports
- Very low engagement in Phase 2
- Clickbait patterns detected

### Format-Specific Optimization

**Text-Only Posts:**
- Highest organic reach potential
- Optimal length: 800-1,300 characters (not too short, not exhausting)
- Use line breaks every 1-2 sentences
- White space improves readability and dwell time
- Emoji as bullet points can help scanability (use sparingly)

**Image Posts:**
- Single image: native upload, bright colors, faces perform well
- Infographics: data-driven visuals get saved and shared
- Multiple images: carousel-like experience
- Alt text improves accessibility and SEO

**Carousel/Document Posts:**
- PDF upload creates swipeable carousel
- 8-12 slides optimal
- First slide = hook/title (most important)
- Last slide = CTA
- Mix text and visuals on each slide
- Highest save rate of any format

**Video Posts:**
- Native video only (no YouTube links)
- Optimal length: 30-90 seconds
- Captions essential (85% watch without sound)
- Vertical or square format preferred
- First 3 seconds must hook viewer

**Polls:**
- High engagement but lower distribution
- 2-3 options perform better than 4
- Duration: 1 week for max engagement
- Follow up with results analysis post
- Great for audience research

**Newsletter/Articles:**
- Lower reach but high authority building
- Searchable long-term (LinkedIn SEO)
- Subscriber notifications drive consistent views
- Complement short posts, don't replace them

### Link Handling

**Critical rule:** External links reduce reach by 40-60%.

**Workarounds:**
1. Post content natively, add link in first comment
2. Post link in first comment and mention "link in comments"
3. Use LinkedIn's native features instead (articles, newsletters, documents)
4. If you must include a link, make the post valuable standalone — the link is a bonus

### Hashtag Strategy

**Best practices:**
- Use 3-5 hashtags per post
- Mix sizes: 1 large (>500K), 1-2 medium (10K-500K), 1-2 small (<10K)
- Place at end of post (not inline)
- Create 1 branded hashtag for your content series
- Research hashtag follower counts before using

### Posting Timing

**Generally optimal (varies by audience):**
- Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday: Highest engagement days
- 7:30-8:30 AM: Pre-work scroll
- 12:00-1:00 PM: Lunch break
- 5:00-6:00 PM: Post-work
- Avoid weekends unless your audience is consumer-focused

**Important:** These are starting points. Actual optimal times depend on your specific audience's timezone and behavior patterns. Always validate with your own data.

### The Golden Hour Rule

The first 60 minutes after posting are critical. To maximize:
1. Post when you can actively monitor and respond
2. Reply to every comment within the first hour
3. Ask questions in replies to spark threads
4. Don't edit the post within the first hour (may reset distribution)
5. Don't immediately share or repost your own content

### SSI (Social Selling Index)

LinkedIn's internal score that affects distribution:
- **Establish your professional brand** (25 pts): Complete profile, publish content
- **Find the right people** (25 pts): Search and connect strategically
- **Engage with insights** (25 pts): Like, comment, share content
- **Build relationships** (25 pts): Connect and message
- Check your SSI at linkedin.com/sales/ssi
