# LinkedIn Post Crafting Reference

Use this reference when writing, editing, or packaging LinkedIn posts.

## Anatomy

### Hook

The first 2 lines appear before "see more" and decide whether the post gets read.

Strong hook patterns:

- Bold statement: "Most LinkedIn advice is wrong. Here's what actually works."
- Contrarian take: "Unpopular opinion: hustle culture is killing innovation."
- Surprising stat: "[Specific percentage] of [audience] struggle with [problem]. But not for the reason you think."
- Personal vulnerability: "I got fired last month. Best thing that ever happened."
- Question: "What's the one skill that 10x'd your career?"
- Pattern interrupt: "Stop scrolling. This might save your next product launch."
- Story opener: "[Timeframe] ago, I misunderstood [problem]."
- List promise: "7 tools that replaced my entire marketing team."

Rules:

- Never start with greetings or filler.
- Create a knowledge gap.
- Make a promise the body delivers.
- Prefer specificity over vague claims.

### Body

- Use one idea per paragraph.
- Keep most paragraphs to 1-2 sentences.
- Use line breaks aggressively.
- Use bullets, arrows, or numbered lists only when they improve scanning.
- Choose the structure that fits the topic: framework, story, contrarian argument, how-to, observation, or list.

### CTA

End with a specific question or action. Avoid generic "Thoughts?" CTAs.

Effective CTA types:

- Specific question.
- Tag invitation.
- Save prompt.
- Share prompt.
- Vote.
- Story request.

## Length

- Short, under 300 characters: hot takes, direct questions, bold statements.
- Medium, 300-1,000 characters: most posts.
- Long, 1,000-3,000 characters: stories, frameworks, deep insights.

Do not pad. Every sentence must earn its place.

## Voice

- Write like a person, but edited.
- Be confident, not arrogant.
- Be specific, not generic.
- Be human, not corporate.
- Have a clear point of view.

Avoid:

- "I'm thrilled to announce..."
- "Excited to share..."
- Standalone "Thoughts?"
- Low-effort engagement bait.
- Excessive hashtags in body text.
- Wall-of-text paragraphs.
